## 1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

**The single biggest value gap, in the market's own words:** *"brilliant musical ideas getting lost in messy
chat threads"* and *"both parties working on different versions"* — the songwriting/worship world has
capture tools (DAWs, notepads) and even real-time editors, but **no calm memory layer that tells you what
changed, lets you recover what was lost, and remembers who helped.** My four surfaces *are* that layer.

**The category-defining fact:** **82% of the 2024 CCLI Top 100 worship songs had ≥2 writers**
([Baptist Standard](https://baptiststandard.com/news/faith-culture/most-hit-worship-songs-are-a-team-effort/)).
Worship songwriting is overwhelmingly **co-written** — so collaboration memory, version safety, and credit are
not edge features, they are the *spine* of the real workflow. COG should not position as "a songwriting app"
(crowded, low willingness-to-pay) but as **the operating memory / system-of-record for collaborative
worship songwriting.** That category is *my lane*.

**The five highest-value moves (ranked, §2):**
1. **Activity "what changed since you left"** = the **retention engine** (the comeback mechanic). Highest frequency, fully automatable, already half-built.
2. **Credits ("the song remembers who helped")** = the **differentiation + monetization + virality** surface. Unserved by every competitor; emotionally urgent in worship; Export = clean Pro gate + growth loop.
3. **Version History (non-technical restore)** = the **trust + churn-prevention + WTP** surface. Removes the #1 creative fear; deep/older history = natural Pro gate; competitors are racing to add it.
4. **People panel** = the **growth-loop anchor** — every collaborator added is an acquisition event; roles make co-writing safe enough to invite more people.
5. **The shared contribution-event contract** = the **moat** — it's what makes all of the above feel "assembled itself," and no competitor has it.

**Why this lane is disproportionately the revenue:** every natural Pro gate in PV09/PV10 lives here — *deep
version history*, *credits export*, and *more collaborators/seats*. Upgrade-trigger research says the
highest-converting moments are **locked-feature click, storage cap, and a collaboration/share event**
([Monetizely](https://www.getmonetizely.com/articles/mastering-freemium-paywalls-strategic-timing-for-saas-success)) —
all three fire inside my surfaces.

## 3. RETENTION-VALUE FINDINGS (weighted first — adapts the skill's "report value" lens to "song-room re-open value")

The skill weights *what makes the recurring artifact get opened, trusted, and forwarded*. For COG that artifact
is **the returning-user moment** (Activity) and **the exportable credit summary** (Credits).

- **RE-OPENED** — The Activity digest is the comeback trigger. Re-engagement that's *behavior-based* (not
  spammy) is the single best-documented retention lever (+50% 1-wk) ([Userpilot](https://userpilot.com/blog/drive-retention/)).
  COG already has the watermark (`markSongSeen`) and serverless recap (`digest-recap`) — so the comeback loop
  is **fully automatable today**. *This is the highest-ROI surface in the entire app for retention.*
- **TRUSTED** — Version History is what lets people *trust the room with serious work*. The market fear is
  explicit: lost work, divergent versions. Restore-that-never-deletes converts fear into commitment → more
  work stored → higher switching cost. Trust features must feel reliable (no confetti/bounce — see persona §7).
- **FORWARDED / RENEWED** — Credits Export is the forward-able artifact. Sending a clean credit summary exposes
  every collaborator to COG (growth loop) and is the moment worth gating to Pro (locked-feature-click =
  documented high-converting trigger). A worship team that *exports credits once* has adopted the system of record.
- **UNDERSTOOD-IN-30s** — All three pass the PVxx "under-three-seconds" bar by design (actor-action-object cards,
  human version language, plain contribution labels). The market punishes the opposite: apps that feel like
  "messy chat threads" or DAW complexity.

## 4. JOBS & TRIGGERS (JTBD switch lines, market-grounded)

- **When** I come back to a shared song after a few days, **I want** to instantly see what my co-writers
  changed and where, **so I can** pick up without re-reading everything or feeling behind. → *Activity*
- **When** I'm about to rework a verse, **I want** to know the old version is safe, **so I can** experiment
  freely instead of hoarding copies. → *Version History* (Andrew Huang's "try it before you judge it" only
  works if nothing is lost — [Music Production Podcast](https://brianfunk.com/blog/andrew-huang)).
- **When** a song we co-wrote gets used or released, **I want** everyone who helped to be remembered fairly,
  **so I can** avoid the awkward "who actually wrote that" conversation. → *Credits* (worship credit norm:
  "lock it down as soon as possible" — [Worship Matters](https://worshipmatters.com/2009/06/24/songwriting-who-gets-the-credit/)).
- **When** I invite someone into a song, **I want** to control what they can do without learning permissions,
  **so I can** share without fear. → *People* (PV07).

## 7. AUTOMATION RISK REGISTER

| Begged-for item | Risk vs. "feels automatic" promise | Resolution |
|---|---|---|
| Credit *accuracy* | Auto-credits could mis-attribute → trust damage | **AUTO-WITH-SETUP**: suggest from accepted events, owner confirms before export (PV10). Never auto-export. |
| "What changed" *summarization* | AI recap could surface private/role-gated content | Server filters by role + emits IDs/kinds only; recap is content-free (already enforced in `activity.ts`). |
| Version *restore* correctness | Silent overwrite = catastrophic | Server-side, additive, conflict-checked, confirmation step (PV09). Never client-assembled. |
| Review/approval queue | Needs a human (owner/pastor) by design | This is *intended* human-in-loop (theological gate) — frame as a feature, not friction. |

## 9. THE VALUE THESIS (one paragraph)

COG's defensible category is **the collaboration system-of-record for worship/Christian songwriting** — and my
four surfaces are that category. Capture/Canvas/Lyric tools have many competitors and low willingness-to-pay;
**memory, recovery, and remembered credit have none and high emotional urgency in a market that is 82%
co-written.** Activity drives the retention that compounds LTV; Version History converts loss-fear into
switching cost; Credits is the unserved, emotionally-charged, exportable Pro surface that *also* spreads the
app every time it's shared; People turns every invite into acquisition. The Pro gates that fund the company
(deep history, credits export, seats) all live in this lane. **Build this lane world-class and COG stops being
"another songwriting app" and becomes the place worship teams cannot afford to leave — because it's the only
place that remembers.**
